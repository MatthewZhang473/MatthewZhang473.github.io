About

I am an ML Engineer at Myrtle.ai, where I develop training pipelines and train automatic speech recognition (ASR) models. Prior to this, I was a ML research assistant at the CBL, Cambridge University. My research interests include Gaussian Processes (GPs), graph machine learning, Monte-Carlo methods, and more recently, linear attention. (See my CV).

My research focuses on developing efficient bayesian inference algorithms that scale to massive graphs with (over 1M nodes!). I am passionate about collaborative research and always interested in new opportunities to work on challenging problems.
